## Key Theoretical Decisions

### Rejection of Magnusson's Peircean taxonomy for instruments
Magnusson classifies instruments as iconic (acoustic), indexical (electronic), symbolic (digital) using Peirce's sign categories. Thomas rejects this because:
- It conflates engineering relationships with semiotic ones (a knob controlling a circuit is not an *index* in Peirce's sense — there's no interpreter)
- Thomas reserves Peircean vocabulary for the **ritual-semiotic layer** where it properly belongs — signs require interpreters
- Magnusson's actual insight (degrees of coupling between gesture and sound) doesn't need Peirce; better described as degrees of **coupling** (tight/loose/arbitrary)

### Peirce over Saussure for music performance
The Saussurean tradition (Nattiez, Tagg, Tarasti, Monelle) is well-established in music semiotics but ill-suited to performance ritual because:
- Dyadic (signifier/signified) vs Peirce's triadic (sign/object/interpretant) — no interpretant means no processual emergence
- Privileges *langue* over *parole* — can't theorise the situated event
- No category for indexicality — the non-arbitrary, causal, felt signs that matter most in performance
- Assumes decoding by competent language user — much of ritual operates below/beside representation
- Synchronic — can't handle becoming/ontogenesis

The comparison has been made in the literature (Turino 2008, Perman 2020, Hernández Salgar 2016, Curry 2011 PhD) — Thomas doesn't need to fight this battle from scratch.

### Under-determined design as the bridge
The degree of indetermination engineered into the instrument at layer 1 directly shapes the indexical field available at layer 3. A fixed instrument → fixed indices. An open machine → emergent indexical ecology. A *distributed* open machine → radically redistributed indexical ecology where audience bodies become nodes.

## Key Concept: Simondon's Philosophy
Gilbert Simondon (1924-1989), French philosopher of technology. Key work: *On the Mode of Existence of Technical Objects* (1958, English translation 2017).

Core concepts relevant to Thomas's work:
- **Concretisation** — technical objects evolve from *abstract* (parts disconnected) toward *concrete* (parts develop reciprocal relationships, internal resonance, plurifunctionality)
- **Technical elements / individuals / ensembles** — hierarchy of technical objects
- **The open machine** (*La machine ouverte*) — a technical object "endowed with a certain *indetermination*" — sensitive to environment, adaptable. **This is Simondon's version of under-determined design.**
- **Associated milieu** — the intermediary space between internal and external milieu that grows as a system concretises
- **Rejection of hylomorphism** — matter is not passive stuff waiting for form to be imposed on it

## Key Concept: Ergodynamics
Coined by Thor Magnusson (2018/2019). From *ergon* (work) + *dynamis* (potential). Describes the total expressive potential of a musical instrument including historical, cultural, subjective dimensions.

Carey (2023) extends this to modular synthesis, identifying:
- **Primary ergodynamics** — the system itself, accumulated over time
- **Secondary ergodynamics** — each individual patch, invented fresh

Connected to Simondon's open machine: patching is technical invention through which new ergodynamics are evolved.
